# Gmail Cold Email Sending Guide — $0 Cost
*Send 15-20 personalized emails/day directly from Gmail. No Instantly, no paid tools.*

---

## Why Gmail Direct Works (When Volume is Low)

Gmail's daily send limit for personal accounts is **500 emails/day**, but for **cold outreach** you should stay at **15-20/day max** because:

1. **Spam detection**: 50+ cold emails/day from one address triggers spam filters
2. **Domain reputation**: One bad batch ruins your primary domain
3. **Personal touch**: Low volume = each email can be more personalized
4. **Reply speed**: You can actually reply to people within 1 hour

**For our scale (50 leads/week, 4-email sequence = 200 emails/week), sending 15-20/day from Gmail is perfect.**

**Total cost: $0**
**Time per day: 10-15 min**

## The Gmail Setup (one-time, 30 min)

### Option A: Use your existing Gmail (simplest)

If your Gmail is `yourname@gmail.com`:
1. Use it as-is
2. Risk: your main email reputation could suffer if you send 100+ cold emails and get spam complaints
3. **Recommendation: only do this if you're OK with some risk to your main email**

### Option B: Create a separate Gmail (safer, $0)

1. Create a new Gmail: `yourname.outreach@gmail.com` or similar
2. Forward incoming replies to your main Gmail
3. Use ONLY for cold email
4. This is your "sending domain" — never use it for personal email

### Option C: Set up a free custom domain (best, $0 first year, then $10/yr)

1. Buy a domain on Namecheap (~$10/yr, $0 first year sometimes with promo)
2. Set up a free Zoho Mail or Proton Mail account on the custom domain
3. Forward replies to your main Gmail
4. Looks more professional (sarah@youragency.com vs sarah.outreach@gmail.com)

**For zero-cost start: Option B (separate Gmail).**

## The Daily Sending Workflow (10-15 min/day)

### Step 1: Open your campaign CSV (2 min)

The file generated by `build_campaign.py` looks like:

```csv
lead_email,lead_first_name,lead_company,step,send_day,subject,body
sarah@notion.so,Sarah,Notion,1,1,quick question Sarah,Hey Sarah, ...
sarah@notion.so,Sarah,Notion,2,3,re: quick question,Hey Sarah, ...
```

Filter to today's send_day in Excel/Google Sheets.

### Step 2: Open Gmail and start a new message (1 min per email)

For each lead, in Gmail:
1. Click **Compose**
2. **To:** the lead's email
3. **Subject:** copy from the CSV
4. **Body:** copy from the CSV (just the body part, not the step number)
5. **Review** for typos or weirdness (especially the {{variables}} replacements)
6. **Send**

**Do not BCC yourself** — looks suspicious. **Do not CC** — looks unprofessional.

### Step 3: Track sends in your spreadsheet (2 min)

Add a column "Date Sent" to your campaign CSV. Update as you send.

### Step 4: Repeat for 15-20 emails per day (10-15 min total)

Don't try to send all 50 in one day. Send 15-20/day over 3 days.

## Tips to Avoid Gmail Spam Filters

### DO ✅

- **Send during business hours** (9am-5pm in recipient's timezone)
- **Send from a real person's name** (not "noreply@")
- **Use plain text** (not HTML email templates)
- **Personalize every email** (the script does this, but review before sending)
- **Vary your send times** (don't send all 20 at 9:01am)
- **Send in batches of 3-5** with 5-10 min breaks between
- **Use a real signature** (your name, title, link to your site)

### DON'T ❌

- **Don't send to "info@" or "sales@"** addresses (high spam score)
- **Don't include images** in the first email (spam trigger)
- **Don't use URL shorteners** (bit.ly, tinyurl — high spam score)
- **Don't use spam words** (free, guaranteed, act now, $$$, !!!, ALL CAPS)
- **Don't send to the same domain more than 5 times per day** (triggers bulk sender detection)
- **Don't send to people who didn't opt-in** (legally required in EU, US, etc.)

## Tracking Replies (CRITICAL)

When someone replies to your cold email, you need to:
1. **Reply within 1 hour** during business hours
2. **Categorize** the reply (positive/objection/unsubscribe/OOO)
3. **Update** the campaign CSV

### Quick reply categorization cheat sheet:

| Reply says... | Category | Action |
|---|---|---|
| "interested", "send info", "let's chat" | POSITIVE | Reply with calendar link |
| "too expensive", "we tried before" | OBJECTION | Send objection-handling reply |
| "remove me", "stop", "unsubscribe" | UNSUBSCRIBE | Add to suppression list |
| "out of office", vacation auto-reply | OOO | Wait 7 days, re-send |
| "wrong person", "ask X" | REFERRAL | Reach out to the referred person |
| Anything unclear | UNCLEAR | Reply asking to clarify |

**Sample positive reply (sent within 1 hour):**
```
Hey {{first_name}},

Great, happy to walk through it.

Here's my calendar for a 15-min fit call: 
calendly.com/outboundsolved/15min

I'll come prepared with a sample campaign for your top 3 
ideal customers so you can see what we'd write.

{{your_name}}
```

**Sample objection reply:**
```
Hey {{first_name}},

Fair question — totally hear you on price.

Most of our clients book 5-15 meetings/mo and their average 
deal size is $5-15K, so the math usually pays for itself in 
the first 1-2 closed deals.

If your average deal size is below $1K, we might not be the 
right fit — happy to refer you elsewhere.

Worth 15 min to see your specific math?

{{your_name}}
```

## The Gmail Tracking Setup (so you can find replies easily)

In Gmail:
1. Create a label: `Cold-Outreach-Replies`
2. Create a filter: If `from:` is in your campaign CSV AND `subject:` contains "re:" → apply label
3. Now all replies to cold emails are auto-labeled and easy to find

How to set this up:
- Gmail Settings → Filters and Blocked Addresses → Create a new filter
- From: `sarah@notion.so OR ivan@linear.app OR ...` (paste your lead emails)
- Subject: contains `re:`
- Apply label: Cold-Outreach-Replies
- Save

## What to Do When You Hit Gmail's Sending Limits

### Soft limits (warnings, not blocks)
- 100+ emails/day from one account → "you're sending a lot of mail" warning
- Reply to that warning, continue
- Usually fine for 1-2 weeks of high sending, then back to normal

### Hard limits (blocks)
- 500 emails/day per Gmail account (rolling 24 hours)
- ~5,000 recipients per rolling 24 hours via SMTP/IMAP

**If you hit a hard limit:**
1. **Wait 24 hours** for the limit to reset
2. **Spread sends** across 2-3 Gmail accounts (use Option B above — separate outreach Gmail)
3. **Lower daily volume** to 10-15/day permanently
