## HOW TO USE THIS PACK

**Don't just create a lead magnet. Create the RIGHT one.**

The best lead magnets:
- Solve a specific, urgent problem
- Can be consumed in 5-10 minutes
- Deliver an immediate "win"
- Make the reader want more
- Position you as the expert

**Before creating a lead magnet, ask:**
- [ ] What's the #1 problem my ideal customer is trying to solve?
- [ ] Can I give them a quick win on this problem?
- [ ] Is the result something I can show in 5-10 minutes?
- [ ] Does solving this make them want my paid solution?

If you can answer "yes" to all 4, you've found a good lead magnet idea.

## PART 1: THE FRAMEWORK

### The 5 Types of Lead Magnets

**Type 1: PDF Guides (Cheap to make, high value)**
- How-to guides
- Industry reports
- Ultimate guides (10,000+ words)
- Checklists
- Swipe files

**Type 2: Calculators (Interactive, very engaging)**
- ROI calculators
- Cost savings calculators
- Time savings calculators
- Salary benchmarks
- Pricing calculators

**Type 3: Templates (Plug-and-play, immediate use)**
- Email templates
- Proposal templates
- Contract templates
- Spreadsheet templates
- Slide deck templates

**Type 4: Tools (Useful resources)**
- Swipe files
- Resource lists
- Calculator spreadsheets
- Checklist PDFs
- Cheat sheets

**Type 5: Quizzes/Assessments (Personalized, viral potential)**
- "What's your X score?"
- "Which Y is right for you?"
- "What's your Z personality?"
- Lead generation quizzes

### The Conversion Math

**Average landing page conversion rate:** 2-5%
**Average email opt-in rate (popup):** 1-3%
**Average content upgrade conversion rate:** 5-15%

**To get 100 subscribers per month, you need:**
- 2,500-5,000 landing page visitors (at 2-5% conversion)
- Or 5,000-10,000 blog readers (at 1-2% popup conversion)
- Or 1,000 page views on a popular post (with content upgrade)

**To get 1,000 subscribers, multiply by 10.**

### The Lead Magnet Quality Test

**Before launching a lead magnet, score it:**

**Does it solve a specific, urgent problem?** (0-5 points)
- 5: Very specific + urgent (e.g., "How to book 10 demos in 30 days")
- 1: Vague + low urgency (e.g., "Marketing tips")

**Can it be consumed in 5-10 minutes?** (0-5 points)
- 5: Yes, quick win (e.g., checklist, calculator)
- 1: No, long read (e.g., 50-page guide)

**Does it deliver an immediate win?** (0-5 points)
- 5: Yes, reader can apply immediately
- 1: No, just information

**Does it make the reader want more?** (0-5 points)
- 5: Yes, clearly leads to paid solution
- 1: No, complete on its own

**Target score: 16-20 out of 20**

If you score below 16, revise.

## PART 7: HOW TO BUILD EACH TYPE

### Building a PDF Guide

**Time:** 2-4 hours
**Tools:** Google Docs, Canva, Adobe InDesign
**Steps:**
1. Pick topic + outline
2. Write 1,000-3,000 words
3. Design in Canva or Google Docs
4. Add visuals (charts, screenshots, diagrams)
5. Add your branding
6. Export as PDF
7. Set up landing page (Carrd, Leadpages, etc.)
8. Set up email delivery (Mailchimp, ConvertKit, etc.)
9. Test the flow
10. Launch + promote

**Pro tip:** Use Canva templates for faster design. Many free lead magnet templates.

### Building a Calculator

**Time:** 4-8 hours
**Tools:** Google Sheets, Airtable, Outgrow, Involve.me
**Steps:**
1. Pick the calculation
2. Gather inputs
3. Build formulas
4. Design UI (in Google Sheets or tool)
5. Add lead capture form
6. Email the results
7. Test all paths
8. Launch

**Pro tip:** Start with Google Sheets (free, simple). Upgrade to a dedicated tool when you have traffic.

### Building a Template Pack

**Time:** 2-4 hours
**Tools:** Google Docs, Google Sheets, Notion
**Steps:**
1. Pick template type (email, proposal, etc.)
2. Create 5-10 templates
3. Make them customizable (placeholders, notes)
4. Add example text
5. Package in a Google Drive folder
6. Set up delivery (zip file or folder link)
7. Create landing page
8. Set up email delivery
9. Test
10. Launch

### Building a Quiz

**Time:** 4-8 hours
**Tools:** Typeform, Outgrow, Involve.me, ScoreApp
**Steps:**
1. Pick topic + outcome
2. Write 10-15 questions
3. Define scoring/results
4. Build in tool
5. Add lead capture
6. Connect to email tool
7. Test all paths
8. Launch

**Pro tip:** Use a quiz tool, not Google Forms. They look more professional and have built-in scoring.

## PART 9: MEASURING SUCCESS

### Key Metrics to Track

**Acquisition:**
- Landing page visits
- Conversion rate (visits → signups)
- Cost per lead (if using paid)
- Signups per channel

**Engagement:**
- Email open rate (welcome email)
- Click-through rate (download link)
- Time to download
- Follow-up email engagement

**Conversion:**
- Lead to MQL rate
- MQL to SQL rate
- SQL to customer rate
- Time to first purchase

### Benchmarks

**Conversion rates (landing page → signup):**
- Weak: <1%
- Average: 2-5%
- Good: 5-10%
- Excellent: 10%+

**Email engagement (welcome sequence):**
- Open rate: 40-60% (welcome emails get high engagement)
- Click rate: 10-20%
- Unsubscribe rate: <1%

**Lead to customer:**
- Cold lead magnet: 0.5-2%
- Warm lead magnet (from email list): 2-5%
- Highly qualified lead magnet: 5-10%

### A/B Testing Ideas

Test these on your landing page:
- Headline (specific vs. vague)
- CTA button text ("Download" vs. "Get the free guide")
- Form length (email only vs. email + name + company)
- Visual design (with image vs. text-only)
- Page length (short vs. long)

## PART 10: COMMON MISTAKES

### Mistake 1: The Generic Lead Magnet
❌ "Marketing tips" (vague, low-value)
✅ "10 cold email subject lines that get 40%+ reply rates" (specific, high-value)

### Mistake 2: The Lengthy Lead Magnet
❌ 50-page ultimate guide (no one reads it)
✅ 5-page checklist (quick win, actually consumed)

### Mistake 3: The Sales-y Lead Magnet
❌ "Why our company is the best" (not a lead magnet)
✅ Solves a specific problem (positions you as expert)

### Mistake 4: The Gated PDF (Locked Content)
❌ "Sign up to see the first 3 pages" (people bounce)
✅ Give the whole thing away (build trust)

### Mistake 5: The Complicated Form
❌ 10 fields (kills conversion)
✅ Email only (or email + first name)

### Mistake 6: The Bad Landing Page
❌ Long-winded intro before the offer
✅ Headline → Subhead → CTA (visible above the fold)

### Mistake 7: No Follow-Up Sequence
❌ One welcome email with the download link
✅ 5-7 email welcome sequence (deliver value, build trust, pitch)

### Mistake 8: The Weak Call-to-Action
❌ "Download our guide" (generic)
✅ "Get the 47-point checklist that helped Client X book 14 demos"

### Mistake 9: No Promotion
❌ Create it and hope people find it
✅ Promote consistently across 3-5 channels

### Mistake 10: No Measurement
❌ "I think it's working"
✅ "We're getting 50 signups/month, 5% click rate, 2% conversion to SQL"
