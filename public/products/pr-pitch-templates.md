# PR Pitch Templates for B2B
## 20 Proven Templates for Getting Featured in Press, Podcasts, and Publications

*For B2B founders, marketing teams, and PR agencies. Step-by-step templates for pitching journalists, podcast hosts, conference organizers, and award committees. Includes follow-up sequences and press release formats.*

## HOW TO USE THIS PACK

**PR works differently than other outreach.** Here's what you need to know:

1. **Journalists get 100+ pitches per day** — yours needs to stand out
2. **Personalization is mandatory** — generic templates get deleted
3. **Newsworthiness matters** — "we exist" isn't news
4. **Follow-up is expected** — most journalists don't respond to first email
5. **Relationships compound** — one good placement leads to more

**Before pitching:**
- [ ] Have a clear, newsworthy story
- [ ] Researched the journalist/publication
- [ ] Read their recent articles
- [ ] Have a compelling hook (not "we launched")
- [ ] Can offer exclusive or insider angle

## PART 1: THE PR MINDSET

### What Journalists Actually Want

**They want stories their readers care about.** Not:
- ❌ "We just raised funding" (unless you're Uber)
- ❌ "We have a new product" (unless it's revolutionary)
- ❌ "We think X is a trend" (everyone thinks that)
- ❌ "We're disrupting the industry" (everyone says that)

**What they DO want:**
- ✅ Data + insight: "We analyzed 1M cold emails and found X"
- ✅ Expert commentary: "Here's what our research shows about Y"
- ✅ Exclusive story: "We're sharing this with you first"
- ✅ Contrarian take: "Why everyone's wrong about Z"
- ✅ Human interest: "How a founder went from $0 to $1M in 90 days"

### The Newsworthiness Test

**Before pitching, score your story:**

**Is it timely?** (0-3 points)
- 3: Tied to current trend/news
- 1: Timely but not urgent

**Is it unique?** (0-3 points)
- 3: Never been done before
- 1: Similar to others

**Is it relevant to their audience?** (0-3 points)
- 3: Perfect fit
- 1: Tangentially related

**Is it quotable?** (0-3 points)
- 3: Has surprising stat or insight
- 1: Generic claim

**Does it have a human angle?** (0-3 points)
- 3: Founder story, customer story, or case study
- 1: Just a product/feature

**Target score: 12-15 = Pitch-ready**
**Score 9-11 = Work on the angle first**
**Score below 9 = Not ready, keep building**

### The "So What?" Test

**Every claim should pass the "so what?" test:**

"We have a new cold email tool."
→ "So what?"

"We have a new cold email tool that helps B2B SaaS founders book 10+ qualified demos per month."
→ "So what?"

"We have a new cold email tool that helps B2B SaaS founders book 10+ qualified demos per month — 3x more than traditional methods — by using AI to personalize at scale."
→ "Now we're talking."

**Always answer "so what?" before pitching.**

## PART 2: JOURNALIST PITCH TEMPLATES (5)

### Template 1: The Data-Driven Pitch

**Best for:** Industry reports, research findings, original data

**Subject:** {{Number}} {{companies/people}} analyzed: {{surprising finding}}

**Body:**
Hi {{journalist_name}},

I read your piece on {{related_topic}} — particularly your point about {{specific_point}}.

We just analyzed {{dataset_size}} {{what_you_analyzed}} and found something surprising: {{one_sentence_finding}}.

For example: {{one_specific_stat_or_example}}.

I thought this might be relevant for your coverage of {{their_beat}}.

Would you be interested in:
- The full data + methodology
- An interview with {{expert_name, our CEO, etc.}}
- Exclusive early access to the report

Happy to send over whatever you need.

Best,
{{your_name}}
{{your_title}}
{{your_company}}

**Example:**
Subject: 10M cold emails analyzed: Reply rates dropped 40% in 2024

Hi Sarah,

I read your piece on cold email trends — particularly your point about inbox placement getting harder.

We just analyzed 10M cold emails sent through our platform in 2024 and found: average reply rates dropped from 4.2% to 2.5% — a 40% decrease.

The top reason: 73% of emails are now landing in spam (up from 31% in 2022).

I thought this might be relevant for your coverage of sales/marketing trends.

Would you be interested in:
- The full data + methodology
- Interview with our head of deliverability
- Exclusive early access to the report

Best,
Alex
Founder, OutboundSolved

**Best for:** Reactive PR (responding to trending news)

**Body:**

Saw your recent piece on {{trending_topic}}.

I work in {{your_field}} and have a different take: {{one_sentence_contrarian_view}}.

The reason: {{brief_explanation_with_data}}.

Happy to elaborate if useful. Also happy to provide a quote or hop on a call.

Best,
{{your_name}}

Best,

**They want great guests who will:**
- Bring unique insights
- Tell compelling stories
- Be entertaining (not boring)
- Promote the episode
- Provide value to their audience

**They DON'T want:**
- ❌ People who just want to promote their product
- ❌ Boring corporate talk
- ❌ People who can't tell stories
- ❌ People who won't promote

## PART 6: NEWSJACKING PITCHES (2)

### What is Newsjacking?

**Newsjacking** = inserting your brand into a trending news story to get media coverage.

**When to do it:**
- A major industry event just happened
- A competitor just made news
- A new regulation was announced
- A viral trend is emerging
- A famous person said something relevant

### Template 16: The Reactive Newsjacking Pitch

**Subject:** Re: {{trending_news_story}} — here's what our data shows

**Body:**
Hi {{journalist_name}},

Saw your piece on {{trending_topic}}.

We have relevant data: {{one_sentence_finding}}.

For example: {{specific_example}}.

I thought this might add to your coverage. Happy to provide:
- The full data
- An interview with {{expert}}
- A quote

Best,
{{your_name}}

### Template 17: The "We're Doing X Differently" Newsjacking Pitch

{{your_name}}

### Email Follow-Up #2 (7-10 days after pitch)

**Subject:** Quick follow-up

**Body:**

Best,

Best,
{{your_name}}

### Key Metrics to Track

**Outreach:**
- Pitches sent
- Response rate (target: 5-10% for cold, 20-30% for warm)
- Open rate (target: 40-60%)
- Follow-up response rate (lower than first email)

**Coverage:**
- Number of placements
- Domain authority of publications
- Estimated reach (publication's monthly readers)
- Sentiment (positive, neutral, negative)
- Backlinks generated

**Business Impact:**
- Referral traffic from coverage
- Leads generated from PR
- Brand search volume increase
- Inbound inquiries

## PART 10: BUILDING A PR LIST

### Where to Find Journalists

**Free sources:**
- Twitter/X (follow journalists in your space)
- LinkedIn (search for "journalist" + your industry)
- Publication websites (look for author bios)
- HARO (Help a Reporter Out) — sign up for daily queries
- Twitter lists (curated by others)
- #journorequest hashtag on Twitter

**Paid sources:**
- Muck Rack (database of 500K+ journalists)
- Cision (database of 1M+ contacts)
- Prowly (PR database + outreach tool)

### How to Qualify a Journalist

**Before pitching, check:**

✅ They cover your topic/industry (read recent articles)
✅ They've written about competitors or similar companies
✅ They have an active Twitter/LinkedIn (engages with sources)
✅ They respond to PR pitches (check their bio or past coverage)
✅ Their publication reaches your target audience

❌ They only cover major brands (you'll get ignored)
❌ They haven't published in months (inactive)
❌ They only write opinion pieces (not interested in news)
❌ Their audience doesn't match yours

### Organizing Your PR List

**Spreadsheet columns:**
- Journalist name
- Publication
- Email
- Twitter handle
- LinkedIn URL
- Topics covered
- Recent articles
- Pitch status (not pitched, pitched, replied, published)
- Notes

**Tools:**
- Airtable (free tier, best for PR tracking)
- Google Sheets (free, simple)
- Notion (free, beautiful)
- Muck Rack (paid, purpose-built)

## PART 11: THE PR PITCH CHECKLIST

Before sending any pitch, verify:

**Your Story:**
- [ ] Is it newsworthy? (Timely, unique, relevant, quotable, human)
- [ ] Does it pass the "so what?" test?
- [ ] Can you explain it in 2 sentences?
- [ ] Is there a hook/angle that makes it different?
- [ ] Do you have data, quotes, or visuals to support it?

**Your Pitch Email:**
- [ ] Subject line under 60 characters
- [ ] Personalized (mentions their work specifically)
- [ ] Under 200 words
- [ ] One clear ask (interview, quote, or coverage)
- [ ] No attachments
- [ ] No images in first email
- [ ] Plain text (not HTML)

**Your Follow-Up Plan:**
- [ ] First follow-up: 3-5 days
- [ ] Second follow-up: 7-10 days
- [ ] Final follow-up: 2 weeks
- [ ] "Different angle" email if no response
- [ ] Polite exit if still no response

**Your Media Kit:**
- [ ] Company logo (high-res)
- [ ] Founder photos
- [ ] Product screenshots
- [ ] Customer testimonials
- [ ] Press coverage (existing)
- [ ] Fact sheet (one-pager)
- [ ] Boilerplate description

## PART 12: COMMON MISTAKES

### Mistake 1: The Generic Pitch
❌ "Hi, I'm from {{company}}. We do {{X}}. Want to cover us?"
✅ "Hi {{name}}, saw your piece on {{topic}}. We have data on {{related}} that might be relevant."

### Mistake 2: The Wall of Text
❌ 500-word pitch with full company history
✅ 150-word pitch with one clear angle

### Mistake 3: The Attachment
❌ Sending a 10-page press release as attachment
✅ Short pitch in body, offer to send more if interested

### Mistake 4: The "We're the Best" Pitch
❌ "We're the leading provider of..."
✅ "We just hit {{specific_milestone}} — here's what we learned"

### Mistake 5: The Desperate Follow-Up
❌ "Please respond, this is really important to us"
✅ "Just following up. If not a fit, no worries."

### Mistake 6: The No-Angle Pitch
❌ "We just launched our new product"
✅ "We just launched X — here's why it's different from Y and Z"

### Mistake 7: The Wrong Journalist
❌ Pitching a tech journalist about your healthcare product
✅ Researching and pitching relevant journalists only

### Mistake 8: The Pitch Timing
❌ Pitching on Friday afternoon or holidays
✅ Pitching Tuesday-Thursday, 9-11am recipient's time

### Mistake 9: The "Spray and Pray"
❌ Sending 100 identical pitches
✅ Sending 20 highly personalized pitches

### Mistake 10: No Relationship Building
❌ Only contacting when you need something
✅ Engaging with their content year-round, being helpful
