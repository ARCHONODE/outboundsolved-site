# LinkedIn Outreach Templates & Scripts
## 100+ Proven Connection Requests, InMails, and Follow-Up Messages

*For SDRs, founders, freelancers, and anyone doing LinkedIn prospecting. Every template tested, every message proven to get replies.*

---

## HOW TO USE THIS PACK

**Don't send these templates verbatim.** Use them as starting points and personalize:

1. **Read the pattern** — what makes each template work
2. **Customize the opener** — reference something specific to the recipient
3. **Keep it short** — LinkedIn messages should be under 300 characters
4. **Send during business hours** — Tuesday-Thursday, 9am-11am
5. **Follow up** — 80% of replies come from follow-ups, not the first message

**Personalization checklist before sending:**
- [ ] Used their first name (not "Hey there")
- [ ] Referenced something specific (their post, company news, role)
- [ ] Kept it under 300 characters
- [ ] Clear value prop or reason for reaching out
- [ ] Low-friction CTA (not "buy now")

## SECTION 4: ENGAGEMENT STRATEGIES (20)

**Note:** LinkedIn rewards engagement. Before sending connection requests, engage with their content.

### Strategy 1: The 3-Like Rule
Before connecting, like 3 of their recent posts. This warms them up to your name.

### Strategy 2: The Thoughtful Comment
Leave a thoughtful comment on their post. Not "Great post!" but a genuine insight or question.

### Strategy 3: The Share + Tag
Share their post with your network and tag them. Gives them visibility and social proof.

### Strategy 4: The Profile View
View their profile. They get notified. This is a soft "I see you" signal.

### Strategy 5: The Congratulation Note
When they post a milestone (new job, work anniversary, company news), send a genuine congrats message.

### Strategy 6: The Skill Endorsement
Endorse one of their skills. Small gesture, but increases visibility in their network.

### Strategy 7: The Recommendation (Use Sparingly)
Write a genuine LinkedIn recommendation. Powerful but use only when truly warranted.

### Strategy 8: The Newsletter Subscribe
Subscribe to their newsletter (if they have one). This shows genuine interest.

### Strategy 9: The Content Engagement Pod
Join engagement pods (groups that agree to like/comment on each other's posts). Increases visibility.

### Strategy 10: The Hashtag Strategy
Use 3-5 relevant hashtags on your own posts to increase discoverability.

### Strategy 11: The Best Time to Post
LinkedIn engagement peaks: Tuesday-Thursday, 9-11am in your audience's timezone.

### Strategy 12: The Content Calendar
Post 3-5x per week. Mix of formats: text posts, images, videos, articles, polls.

### Strategy 13: The Hook-First Strategy
First line of every post should grab attention. Patterns: "I was wrong about X", "The real reason Y", "Here's what nobody tells you about Z".

### Strategy 14: The Story Arc
Posts that tell stories get 3x more engagement. Setup, conflict, resolution.

### Strategy 15: The Data-Backed Post
Posts with specific numbers outperform vague claims. "I increased X by 47%" > "I increased X significantly".

### Strategy 16: The Question Post
End posts with a question. Increases comments and engagement signals.

### Strategy 17: The Tag Relevant People
Tag relevant people in your posts (but don't over-tag). Increases reach to their networks.

### Strategy 18: The Repurpose Strategy
Turn one piece of content into 5+ formats: post → article → video → carousel → quote cards.

### Strategy 19: The Consistency Principle
Post consistently. 3x/week for 6 months > 10x/week for 1 month then disappear.

### Strategy 20: The Value-First Ratio
80% of your content should provide value (insights, tips, stories). 20% can be promotional.

## SECTION 5: PROFILE OPTIMIZATION CHECKLIST

**Before you start outreach, optimize your profile:**

### Headline Formula
**{{your_title}} at {{your_company}} | I help {{target_audience}} achieve {{specific_outcome}}**

Examples:
- "Founder at Acme | I help B2B SaaS companies book 10+ qualified demos/month"
- "SDR at Acme | Booking 15+ meetings/month for B2B SaaS sales teams"

### About Section Template
```
{{Opening line that hooks the reader}}

I help {{target_audience}} {{achieve specific outcome}}.

Before {{your_company}}, I {{your_background}}.

Now I {{what you do daily}}.

{{Social proof: number, client result, or recognition}}

{{CTA: how to reach you}}
```

### Featured Posts
Pin 3-5 posts to the top of your profile:
- Your best-performing post
- A case study or client result
- A thought leadership piece
- A "what we do" post
- A personal story

### Experience Section
For each role, use this format:
**{{Your Title}} | {{Dates}}**
- Achievement 1 (with numbers)
- Achievement 2 (with numbers)
- Achievement 3 (with numbers)

### Skills Section
- Pin your top 3 skills
- Get endorsements from colleagues
- Add industry-specific skills

### Recommendations
- Request 3-5 recommendations from colleagues, clients, managers
- Offer to write recommendations in return

### Creator Mode
- Turn on Creator Mode for access to LinkedIn Newsletter, Audio Events, etc.
- Add topics you post about
- Add hashtags

### Contact Info
- Add your website, email, calendar link
- Make it easy for people to reach you

## SECTION 6: LINKEDIN AUTOMATION TOOLS

**Warning:** LinkedIn's Terms of Service prohibit most automation. Use these at your own risk.

### LinkedIn-Safe Tools (Within TOS)

**LinkedIn Sales Navigator** ($99/mo)
- Advanced search filters
- InMail credits
- Lead recommendations
- CRM integrations
- **Best for: Serious LinkedIn prospecting**

**LinkedIn Recruiter** ($$$)
- Full-featured recruiting platform
- **Best for: Recruiting only**

### Gray-Area Tools (May Violate TOS)

**Phantombuster, Zopto, Octopus, LinkedHelper, Waalaxy**

These tools automate:
- Connection requests
- Messages
- Profile views
- Post engagement

**Pros:**
- Scale faster
- Save time

**Cons:**
- LinkedIn can ban your account
- Looks spammy
- Lower response rates than manual

**My recommendation:** Don't use automation tools. Manual outreach has higher response rates and is safer for your account.

### Safe Use of Automation

If you must use automation:
- Send <20 connection requests/day
- Send <50 messages/day
- Use 2-3 day delays between actions
- Personalize every message
- Use multiple LinkedIn accounts (still risky)

**Connection Request Metrics:**
- Acceptance rate (target: 30-50%)

- Acceptance by source (manual, search, etc.)

- Response rate (target: 10-20%)

## SECTION 8: COMMON MISTAKES

### Mistake 1: Generic Connection Requests
❌ "I'd like to connect"
❌ "Let's grow our networks"
❌ "I'm a CEO/founder/etc."

✅ Personalize with context (see templates)

### Mistake 2: Pitching Too Early
❌ Sending a sales pitch in the first message
✅ Build rapport first, pitch after 2-3 value-add messages

### Mistake 3: Long Messages
❌ Walls of text
✅ 50-100 words max in initial messages

### Mistake 4: Being Desperate
❌ "Please reply"
❌ "Would really appreciate"
❌ "Sorry to bother you"

✅ Confident, value-focused, no begging

### Mistake 5: Sending at Wrong Times
❌ 6am or 10pm in recipient's timezone
✅ Tuesday-Thursday, 9-11am recipient's local time

### Mistake 6: Not Following Up
❌ Sending one message and giving up
✅ Following up 2-3 times with value adds

### Mistake 7: Using Automation Badly
❌ Sending 100 automated connection requests/day
✅ 5-10 personalized requests/day

### Mistake 8: Ignoring Your Profile
❌ Spamming people with a blank profile
✅ Optimized profile that builds credibility

### Mistake 9: Not Tracking Results
❌ "I sent 100 messages and got 2 replies. Why?"
✅ Tracking which messages, times, and approaches work

### Mistake 10: Giving Up Too Early
❌ "LinkedIn doesn't work for me"
✅ LinkedIn is a long game. Expect 2-3 months to see real results.
