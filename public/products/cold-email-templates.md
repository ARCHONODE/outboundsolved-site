## SECTION 1: FOUNDATION (The Building Blocks)

### Subject Line Formulas That Work

These subject lines have been tested across thousands of B2B SaaS cold emails. The pattern: short, specific, curiosity-driven.

1. `quick question, {{first_name}}`
2. `noticed {{company}} is doing {{thing}}`
3. `{{first_name}} - saw your {{post/hiring/feature}}`
4. `re: {{original_subject}}`
5. `{{first_name}} - one stat for you`
6. `closing the loop, {{first_name}}`
7. `{{similar_company}} booked {{number}} meetings in week 1`
8. `last email from me`
9. `{{first_name}} - saw this and thought of {{company}}`
10. `quick thought on {{company}}'s {{topic}}`

### First-Line Hooks (The Pattern Interrupt)

Your first line after the greeting must NOT be:
- "I hope this email finds you well" (generic)
- "I'm reaching out because..." (self-centered)
- "We help companies like yours..." (salesy)

Instead, use one of these patterns:

- **Specific observation:** "Noticed {{company}} just launched {{feature}}."
- **Insider reference:** "Most {{their_role}} I talk to say..."
- **Pattern interrupt:** "Saw {{specific_thing_about_them}} — looked like {{implication}}."
- **Direct value:** "Found this stat interesting: {{relevant_data_point}}."

### CTA Variations (The Ask)

End with ONE clear, low-friction ask:

- "Worth a 15-min look at whether it'd work for {{company}}?"
- "Happy to send a sample of what we'd write for {{company}} if useful."
- "Open to a quick conversation this week?"
- "Want me to send over the case study?"

### Sign-Off Patterns

Keep it simple and human:

- "{{your_name}}"
- "The {{your_company}} team"
- "Best, {{your_name}}"
- "{{your_name}} | {{title}}"

### Prompt 2: Write Your Own Sequence

```
You are a B2B cold email expert. Write a 4-email sequence for:

Product: {{your_product}}
Target: {{your_target_audience}}
Pain point: {{their_pain_point}}
Value prop: {{your_value_prop}}
Proof: {{your_proof_point}}

Structure:
- Day 1: Initial outreach (pattern interrupt)
- Day 3: Follow-up (value drop)
- Day 7: Follow-up (case study)
- Day 12: Breakup

Keep emails under 100 words. Use plain text. No marketing speak.
```

### Prompt 3: A/B Test Subject Lines

```
You are a B2B cold email expert. Generate 5 subject line variations for:

Email topic: {{topic}}
Target audience: {{target_audience}}
Goal: {{open_rate_goal}}

Use these patterns:
- Pattern interrupt
- Specific observation
- Curiosity gap
- Direct value
- Social proof

Return 5 subject lines, each with a brief explanation of why it would work.
```

### Prompt 4: Personalize at Scale

```
You are a B2B cold email expert. Personalize this email for 10 different leads:

Base template:
[PASTE TEMPLATE]

Leads:
[PASTE 10 LEADS WITH NAME, COMPANY, TITLE, RECENT ACTIVITY]

For each lead, generate a customized version that:
- Opens with a specific observation
- References their role/company
- Has a clear CTA
- Stays under 100 words

Return 10 personalized emails, one per lead.
```

```
You are a B2B cold email expert. Write a case study based on:

Industry: {{industry}}
Size: {{size}}
Challenge: {{their_challenge}}
Solution: {{what_you_did}}
Result: {{results_with_numbers}}

Format:
- One-line summary
```

## HOW TO USE THIS PACK

1. **Pick a template** that fits your use case
2. **Customize the variables** ({{first_name}}, {{company}}, etc.)
3. **Use the AI prompts** to personalize at scale
4. **Test subject lines** — never send without testing 2-3 variants
5. **Follow up** — 80% of replies come from follow-ups, not the first email
6. **Track everything** — open rates, reply rates, meetings booked
7. **Iterate** — use what works, kill what doesn't
