## PART 1: THE MINDSET

### Cold Calling in 2026: Still Worth It?

**Yes, but differently than 2015.**

What works today:
- ✅ Shorter calls (under 3 minutes)
- ✅ Value-first approach (give before asking)
- ✅ Multi-channel (call + email + LinkedIn)
- ✅ Personalization (research before calling)
- ✅ Soft asks (not "buy now")

What doesn't work:
- ❌ Long pitches (45-second monologues)
- ❌ Aggressive closes ("When can I put you down for?")
- ❌ Cold calls with no research
- ❌ Voicemails longer than 20 seconds
- ❌ Multiple calls without value

### The 3 Goals of Every Cold Call

**Goal 1: Get the meeting.** (Not make the sale)
- Aim for 15-min Zoom or in-person
- Don't pitch, just confirm the meeting

**Goal 2: Get permission to follow up.**
- "Can I send you some info via email?"
- "Mind if I follow up next week?"

**Goal 3: Get a referral.**
- "Who else at {{company}} should I talk to?"
- "Is there someone on your team who handles this?"

**If you achieve ANY of these 3, the call was successful.**

### The Psychology of Cold Calls

**The prospect is expecting:**
- A generic pitch
- To be pressured
- To waste their time

**Your job:** Be different.

- Be human, not scripted
- Be brief, not pushy
- Be helpful, not salesy
- Be curious, not assumptive

**When you call, imagine:**
- You're calling a friend you haven't talked to in years
- You're catching up, not pitching
- You're offering value, not asking for favors

## PART 2: PRE-CALL PREPARATION

### Research Checklist (5-10 minutes per prospect)

**Before you dial:**

1. **Check LinkedIn** (2 min)
   - Their recent posts
   - Their role/responsibilities
   - Shared connections
   - Career history

2. **Check company website** (2 min)
   - Recent news/press releases
   - Product pages
   - Team page
   - About page

3. **Check funding/news** (2 min)
   - Crunchbase for recent funding
   - Google News for company mentions
   - LinkedIn for company updates

4. **Find a trigger event** (2 min)
   - New hire in their team
   - Product launch
   - Funding round
   - Expansion/new office
   - New partnership

5. **Check for mutual connections** (1 min)
   - LinkedIn mutual connections
   - Mention in opener for warm intro feel

### The "I Noticed..." Framework

Your opener should reference **something specific** you noticed:

**Good triggers:**
- "I saw you just launched {{product}}"
- "I noticed you posted about {{topic}}"
- "Congrats on the {{funding_round}}"
- "Saw {{company}} is hiring for {{role}}"
- "I read your recent article on {{topic}}"

**Why this works:** Shows you did research, makes it personal, gives them a reason to engage.

## PART 3: THE OPENER (First 10 Seconds)

The opener has ONE job: get past the gatekeeper / get them to engage for 3 more seconds.

### The 3-Part Opener Formula

**Pattern:** Name + Reason + Permission

1. **Name:** "Hi {{first_name}}"
2. **Reason:** "I'm calling because {{specific_reason}}"
3. **Permission:** "Did I catch you at a bad time?"

### Pattern 1: The Direct Opener

"Hi {{first_name}}, this is {{your_name}} from {{your_company}}. I'm calling because I noticed {{company}} just {{trigger_event}}, and I help {{target_companies}} with {{value_prop}}. Did I catch you at a bad time?"

**Why it works:** Specific, respectful, asks for permission to continue.

### Pattern 2: The Referral Opener

"Hi {{first_name}}, this is {{your_name}}. {{mutual_connection}} suggested I reach out. I'm calling about {{topic}}. Did I catch you at a bad time?"

**Why it works:** Social proof (referral), specific reason, permission ask.

### Pattern 3: The Trigger Event Opener

"Hi {{first_name}}, this is {{your_name}} from {{your_company}}. I saw {{trigger_event}} at {{company}}, and thought this might be relevant. Got 30 seconds?"

**Why it works:** Event-based, time-bounded, low-pressure ask.

### Pattern 4: The Value-First Opener

"Hi {{first_name}}, this is {{your_name}}. I just put together a {{resource_type}} specifically for {{target_audience}} in {{industry}}. Thought you might find it useful. Got 30 seconds to tell you about it?"

**Why it works:** Leads with value, not pitch. They might actually say yes.

### Pattern 5: The Question Opener

"Hi {{first_name}}, this is {{your_name}} from {{your_company}}. Quick question: how is {{company}} currently handling {{specific_challenge}}?"

**Why it works:** Engages them immediately with a question. Skips the pitch.

### Bad Openers (Don't Do This)

❌ "Hi, is this {{name}}? How are you today?" (Generic, wastes time)
❌ "I'm calling from {{company}}, we're the leading provider of..." (Pitch too early)
❌ "Did you receive my email?" (Defensive)
❌ "I know you're busy, so I'll be brief" (Apologetic)
❌ "I'm calling to introduce myself" (Pointless)

### Good vs. Bad Examples

**Bad opener (generic):**
"Hi Sarah, this is Alex from Acme. We're a leading B2B SaaS platform that helps companies like yours with sales. Do you have 5 minutes?"

**Good opener (specific + value):**
"Hi Sarah, this is Alex from OutboundSolved. I saw Acme just hired three AEs — congrats on the growth. I help SaaS companies like yours book 10+ qualified demos per month via cold email. Did I catch you at a bad time?"

## PART 4: THE PITCH (Seconds 10-60)

If they say "no" to "did I catch you at a bad time," you hang up. Don't argue.

If they engage, you have **~50 seconds** to:
1. Explain why you're calling (1 sentence)
2. Explain what you do (1 sentence)
3. Show relevance (1 sentence)
4. Ask a question (1 sentence)

### The BANT Framework

**Use BANT to structure your pitch:**

- **B**udget: Do they have budget for this?
- **A**uthority: Are they the decision-maker?
- **N**eed: Do they have the problem you solve?
- **T**imeline: When are they looking to solve it?

**Example pitch using BANT:**
"Quick context: I help B2B SaaS founders book 10+ qualified demos per month. I noticed Acme is hiring AEs, so I'm guessing you're investing in growth. How are you currently generating demos for them?"

**This is 3 sentences. That's it.**

### The Pattern: Problem → Solution → Question

**Formula:**
1. **Problem** (1 sentence): "Most {{target_role}} struggle with {{problem}}."
2. **Solution** (1 sentence): "We help {{target_audience}} {{solve_problem}}."
3. **Question** (1 sentence): "Is that something you're exploring right now?"

**Real example:**
"Most B2B SaaS founders struggle to book 10+ qualified demos per month from organic channels alone. We help them set up outbound systems that generate pipeline on autopilot. Is that something you're exploring right now?"

### The "If You're Not the Right Person" Move

If they say "I'm not the right person" or "We already have a solution":

"Is there someone on your team who handles {{topic}}? I don't want to bother you if it's not relevant."

**Why this works:** Respectful, gets you a referral instead of a dead end.

## PART 5: HANDLING COMMON OBJECTIONS

### Objection 1: "I'm not interested"

**Response:** "Totally fair. Mind if I ask — is it because you're already happy with {{current_solution}}, or is it just not a priority right now?"

**Why it works:** Doesn't argue. Asks for the REAL reason. Opens dialogue.

### Objection 2: "We already have a solution"

**Response:** "Makes sense. How's that working out for you? Not trying to replace what you have — just curious."

**Why it works:** Acknowledges, asks a question, doesn't compete.

### Objection 3: "Not the right time"

**Response:** "Totally get it. When does it make sense to revisit? I can ping you in {{timeframe}}."

**Why it works:** Acknowledges, sets a follow-up, stays top of mind.

### Objection 4: "Send me some info"

**Response:** "Happy to. Mind if I ask — what's the best email? And while I send that, what would make this worth a 15-min call next week?"

**Why it works:** Confirms email, but also tries to book meeting. Most "send me info" means "I want to get off the phone." Push for the meeting.

### Objection 5: "How much does it cost?"

**Response:** "Depends on what you need. Most clients invest between $X and $Y per month. But before I quote, can I ask a few questions to see if we're even a fit?"

**Why it works:** Gives a range, but doesn't commit. Asks qualifying questions.

### Objection 6: "We don't have budget"

**Response:** "Totally understand. Is that a 'not right now' or a 'not in the foreseeable future'?"

**Why it works:** Distinguishes between timing issue and budget issue. If timing, follow up later.

### Objection 7: "I'm in a meeting / busy / call back later"

**Response:** "Of course. When's a better time? I can call back {{day}} at {{time}}."

**Why it works:** Specific follow-up time = much more likely to actually happen.

### Objection 8: "Just send me an email"

**Response:** "Will do. What would make this worth a call? Just so I send the right info."

**Why it works:** Keeps the door open for a meeting. Most prospects won't answer this honestly, but it shows you care.

### Objection 9: "I'm not the decision-maker"

**Response:** "Totally get it. Who should I talk to about {{topic}}? I don't want to bother the wrong person."

**Why it works:** Gets you a referral. Most people will give you a name.

### Objection 10: "I need to think about it"

**Response:** "Makes sense. What specifically do you want to think about? Maybe I can answer that right now."

**Why it works:** Identifies the real objection hiding behind "need to think."

### Objection 11: "We're not looking right now"

**Response:** "Got it. When does it become a priority? Happy to circle back then."

**Why it works:** Sets a follow-up. Most "not right now" means "not now" not "never."

### Objection 12: "Call me back in 6 months"

**Response:** "Sure, I'll put it on my calendar. Mind if I send you a quick email to confirm the date?"

**Why it works:** Confirms the follow-up. Email also keeps you top of mind.

### Objection 13: "How did you get my number?"

**Response:** "{{source}}. If you'd prefer I don't call again, no problem — just let me know."

**Why it works:** Honest, respectful. Gives them an out if they want it.

### Objection 14: "I'm hanging up / not interested / stop calling"

**Response:** "Understood. Sorry to bother you. Have a good day."

**Why it works:** Graceful exit. Don't argue, don't beg. Move on.

### The Meta-Rule for Objections

**Never argue. Never beg. Always:**

1. **Acknowledge** ("That's fair" / "I understand")
2. **Clarify** ("Is it X or Y?")
3. **Redirect** ("Here's what we could do...")
4. **Confirm next step** ("Should I follow up next week?")

## PART 6: VOICEMAIL SCRIPTS (10 Variations)

### Why Voicemails Matter

- 80% of cold calls go to voicemail
- A good VM = callback rate of 5-10%
- A bad VM = hung up before you finish

**Best practice:** Leave a 15-20 second VM with a callback number. Don't pitch.

### VM 1: The Direct Callback
"Hi {{first_name}}, this is {{your_name}} from {{your_company}}. I noticed {{trigger_event}} at {{company}}, and thought this might be relevant. My number is {{phone}}. Thanks."

### VM 2: The Curiosity Hook
"Hi {{first_name}}, {{your_name}} here. I just put together a {{resource_type}} for {{target_audience}} in {{industry}}. Thought you might find it useful. {{phone}} if you want a copy. Thanks."

### VM 3: The Referral
"Hi {{first_name}}, {{mutual_connection}} suggested I reach out. {{your_name}} from {{your_company}}. Quick question about {{topic}} when you have 5 min: {{phone}}. Thanks."

### VM 4: The Event Reference
"Hi {{first_name}}, saw {{company}}'s {{event}}. {{your_name}}, calling from {{your_company}}. Got 30 sec to tell you why I'm calling? {{phone}}."

### VM 5: The Pattern Interrupt
"Hi {{first_name}}, this is going to sound like every other cold call you've gotten this week, but it's not. {{your_name}} from {{your_company}}. Here's why I'm calling: {{one_sentence_value_prop}}. {{phone}} if interested. If not, no worries. Thanks."

### VM 6: The Question
"Hi {{first_name}}, {{your_name}} here. Quick question: how is {{company}} currently handling {{specific_challenge}}? I have some thoughts. {{phone}}. Thanks."

### VM 7: The Time-Bound
"Hi {{first_name}}, {{your_name}}. I know you're busy, so I'll be brief. Two things: {{one_sentence_value_prop}}, and {{one_sentence_proof_point}}. If interested, {{phone}}. Thanks."

### VM 8: The Specific Number
"Hi {{first_name}}, {{your_name}} from {{your_company}}. I helped {{similar_company}} {{specific_result}} in {{timeframe}}. Thought you might want to know how. {{phone}}. Thanks."

### VM 9: The Soft Pitch
"Hi {{first_name}}, this is {{your_name}}. I'm calling because {{one_sentence_observation_about_their_company}}. I might be able to help with {{related_challenge}}. {{phone}} if you want to chat. No pressure. Thanks."

### VM 10: The Brief and Exit
"Hi {{first_name}}, {{your_name}}. Calling from {{your_company}}. One sentence: we help {{target_companies}} {{value_prop}}. {{phone}}. Thanks."

### Voicemail Best Practices

✅ Keep it under 20 seconds
✅ Say your name + company clearly
✅ Say your phone number twice
✅ Don't pitch
✅ Don't sound desperate

❌ Don't leave a 60-second VM
❌ Don't include your email (they won't remember it)
❌ Don't use jargon
❌ Don't be apologetic ("Sorry to bother you")

## PART 7: DISCOVERY QUESTIONS (BANT + CHAMP)

### The BANT Framework

**B - Budget:**
- "What's your budget for {{topic}} this year?"
- "How are you currently budgeting for {{specific_function}}?"
- "What would be a reasonable investment for {{solution}}?"

**A - Authority:**
- "Who else needs to be involved in this decision?"
- "Are you the right person to evaluate {{solution}}?"
- "Who would need to sign off on this?"

**N - Need:**
- "What's the biggest challenge with {{topic}} right now?"
- "How is this affecting your team/business?"
- "What happens if you don't solve this in the next 6 months?"

**T - Timeline:**
- "When are you looking to have this solved by?"
- "What's driving the timeline?"
- "Is this Q1 priority or Q2?"

### The CHAMP Framework (Alternative)

**C - Challenges:**
- "What are your biggest challenges with {{topic}}?"
- "What's keeping you up at night?"

**H - Authority:**
- "Who else is involved in this decision?"
- "Who needs to approve this?"

**A - Money:**
- "What's your budget for {{solution}}?"
- "How are you investing in this area currently?"

**M - Prioritization:**
- "How important is solving this right now?"
- "What are your other priorities?"

**P - (CHAMP is the framework, P can be "Plan")**
- "What does success look like for you?"
- "What's your ideal outcome?"

### The MEDDIC Framework (For Complex Sales)

**M - Metrics:**
- "How would you measure success?"
- "What KPIs matter most?"

**E - Economic Buyer:**
- "Who has the final say on this?"
- "Who controls the budget?"

**D - Decision Criteria:**
- "What would you evaluate before deciding?"
- "What's most important to you?"

**D - Decision Process:**
- "What does your decision process look like?"
- "How long does it typically take?"

**I - Identify Pain:**
- "What's the biggest pain point you're trying to solve?"
- "What happens if you don't solve this?"

**C - Champion:**
- "Who on your team would champion this internally?"
- "Who would be your internal advocate?"

### Active Listening Phrases

To show you're listening (not just waiting to talk):
- "That's interesting. Tell me more about that."
- "So if I understand correctly, you're saying..."
- "What I'm hearing is... is that right?"
- "That makes sense. What's driving that?"
- "Got it. And how does that affect {{business_outcome}}?"

## PART 8: CLOSING THE CALL

### The Soft Close

**Goal:** Get the meeting, not the sale.

**Pattern 1: The Direct Ask**
"Based on what you've shared, I think we could really help. Would it make sense to schedule 15 minutes next week to explore this?"

**Pattern 2: The Calendar Link**
"I can send you a calendar link so you can pick a time that works. What's your email?"

**Pattern 3: The Value Justification**
"Most of our clients see {{result}} in the first 30 days. Worth a 15-min conversation to see if we can do the same for {{company}}?"

**Pattern 4: The Multi-Stakeholder**
"Should we include anyone else from your team in the next conversation?"

### If They Say "Yes"

✅ Confirm date and time
✅ Send calendar invite immediately
✅ Send a short follow-up email confirming the meeting
✅ Ask if there's anything specific they want to discuss

### If They Say "Let Me Think About It"

✅ "Makes sense. What specifically do you want to think about?"
✅ "Is it a budget thing, a timing thing, or something else?"
✅ "Would it help if I sent over {{case_study, ROI_calculator, etc.}?"
✅ "When's a good time to follow up?"

### If They Say "Send Me Some Info"

✅ "Happy to. What's the best email?"
✅ "What specifically would be most useful? A case study, pricing, a demo video?"
✅ "And while I send that, should we pencil in 15 min next week to walk through it together?"

### If They Say "Call Me Back in X Weeks"

✅ "Sure, I'll put it on my calendar for {{specific_date}}. Mind if I send a quick email to confirm?"
✅ "Anything specific you'd want me to bring to that conversation?"

## PART 9: CALL STRUCTURE TEMPLATES

### Template 1: The 3-Minute Call (Standard)

**0:00-0:10 - Opener:**
"Hi {{first_name}}, this is {{your_name}} from {{your_company}}. I noticed {{trigger_event}}, and I help {{target_companies}} with {{value_prop}}. Did I catch you at a bad time?"

**0:10-0:30 - Pitch:**
"Quick context: most {{target_role}} struggle with {{problem}}. We help by {{solution}}. Companies like {{similar_company}} have seen {{result}}."

**0:30-2:00 - Discovery:**
"Couple of questions: How are you currently handling {{topic}}? What's working? What's not? If we could {{solution}}, what would that be worth to {{company}}?"

**2:00-2:30 - Value Proposition:**
"Based on what you've shared, I think we could help. Here's what we'd cover in a 15-min call: {{three_bullet_points}}."

**2:30-3:00 - Close:**
"Worth exploring? I can send a calendar link."

### Template 2: The 5-Minute Call (Deeper Discovery)

**0:00-0:30 - Opener + Pitch:**
[Same as Template 1]

**0:30-3:30 - Deep Discovery:**
- Current state: "Walk me through how you currently {{process}}"
- Pain points: "What's the biggest bottleneck?"
- Impact: "How is that affecting {{metric, team, business}}?"
- Decision process: "If you were to solve this, what would the decision process look like?"
- Other stakeholders: "Who else would be involved in evaluating {{solution}}?"
- Timeline: "When are you looking to have this resolved?"

**3:30-4:30 - Value Proposition:**
"Here's how we'd approach this: {{three_bullet_points}}. Most clients see {{result}} in {{timeframe}}."

**4:30-5:00 - Close:**
"Makes sense to dive deeper? I can send a calendar link for next week."

### Template 3: The Gatekeeper Call

**0:00-0:10 - Opener:**
"Hi, this is {{your_name}} from {{your_company}}. I'm trying to reach {{decision_maker_name}}. Are they available?"

**If yes:** "Thanks. [Wait for transfer]"

**If no (gatekeeper won't transfer):**
"Could you let them know {{your_name}} called? I'll send a follow-up email. What's the best email?"

**If gatekeeper asks "What's this about?":**
"It's about {{one_sentence_topic}}. I'd like to send them some info. Can you confirm their email?"

## PART 10: ADVANCED STRATEGIES

### The "Triple Touch" Sequence

**Day 1: Call (leave VM)**
- Use VM Script #1 (Direct Callback)
- 15-20 seconds max

**Day 2: Email (soft intro)**
- Subject: "Tried to reach you"
- Body: 2-3 sentences + calendar link
- Don't pitch

**Day 5: Call (different time of day)**
- Try morning if first was afternoon
- Use VM Script #2 (Curiosity Hook)

**Day 8: LinkedIn connect + message**
- Connect with personal note
- After acceptance, send InMail

**Day 12: Call (final attempt)**
- Use VM Script #5 (Pattern Interrupt)
- If no answer, send breakup email

**Day 15: Breakup email**
- "I've tried reaching out a few times. If timing isn't right, totally get it. Here's my calendar if anything changes: {{link}}"

### The "Referral Chain" Strategy

**Instead of asking "Who handles this?" ask:**
"Who ELSE at {{company}} would be good for me to talk to about {{topic}}? I want to make sure I'm talking to the right people."

**Why it works:**
- Asking for ONE referral gets you ONE name
- Asking for MULTIPLE referrals gets you 2-3 names
- You're not "passing the buck" — you're "doing thorough research"

### The "Trigger Event" Calendar

**Set Google Alerts for:**
- Company name + "hires" / "launches" / "raises" / "expands"
- Industry keywords + "news"
- Competitor mentions
- Key executive moves

**When you see a trigger event:**
1. Call within 24 hours (most relevant)
2. Reference the event in your opener
3. Show you were paying attention

### The "Voicemail + Email" Combo

**Day 1: Leave VM (15-20 sec)**
- Brief, clear, callback number
- No pitch

**Day 1 (2 hours later): Send email**
- Subject: "Tried to reach you"
- Body: "Just left you a VM. Quick context: {{one_sentence_value_prop}}. If interested: {{calendar_link}}"
- Keep it under 100 words

**Why it works:** They see the VM in missed calls, then see your email. Multi-channel = harder to ignore.

### The "Champion Building" Strategy

**Find your internal champion:**
- Someone who benefits from the solution
- Has influence with the decision-maker
- Will advocate for you internally

**Questions to identify the champion:**
- "Who on your team would be most affected by this?"
- "Who would be evaluating this internally?"
- "If we did this, who would manage the relationship?"

**Once identified, nurture them:**
- Send them useful content (not just sales)
- Ask for their advice
- Make them look good internally

## PART 11: MEASURING SUCCESS

### Key Metrics to Track

**Daily:**
- Calls made
- Conversations (actual human convos, not voicemails)
- Conversations to meetings (target: 10-20%)

**Weekly:**
- Calls → Conversations rate (target: 30-50%)
- Conversations → Meetings rate (target: 10-20%)
- Meetings → Opportunities (target: 50-70%)

**Monthly:**
- Cost per qualified meeting
- Average deal size
- Close rate
- Revenue generated

### Dialer Best Practices

**Power dialer (Aircall, Aircall, Orum, etc.):**
- 100-200 dials/day
- 30-50% connection rate
- 5-10 meetings booked/day (if you're good)

**Manual dialing:**
- 50-80 dials/day
- 30-50% connection rate
- 2-5 meetings booked/day

### Call Recording & Analysis

**Record calls (with permission).** Then:
1. Listen to your best calls (what worked?)
2. Listen to your worst calls (what didn't?)
3. Share with your team (collective learning)
4. Identify patterns (common objections, successful pitches)

**Tools:**
- **Gong** — Records, transcribes, analyzes
- **Chorus** — Similar to Gong
- **Aircall** — Built-in recording
- **Outreach** — Sales engagement with call recording

### A/B Testing

Test these variables:
- Opener style (direct vs. value-first)
- Time of day (9am vs. 2pm)
- Day of week (Tue vs. Thu)
- Voicemail length (15 sec vs. 30 sec)
- Call-to-action (meeting vs. follow-up email)

**Run each test for 2 weeks minimum** to get statistically significant data.

## PART 12: COMMON COLD CALLING MISTAKES

### Mistake 1: The Monologue Pitch
❌ Talking for 60 seconds straight
✅ Pitch in 15-20 seconds, then ask a question

### Mistake 2: The Resume Approach
❌ "I'm {{your_name}} from {{your_company}}, we do {{X}}, {{Y}}, and {{Z}}..."
✅ "I'm calling because {{one_sentence_reason}}. Did I catch you at a bad time?"

### Mistake 3: The Fake Rapport
❌ "How are you today? Great! Love the weather..."
✅ Get to the point. They know it's a cold call.

### Mistake 4: The Desperate Close
❌ "Can I send you some info? Please? I'd really appreciate it."
✅ "I can send a case study. What's the best email?"

### Mistake 5: The Feature Dump
❌ Listing all 47 features of your product
✅ Focus on 1-2 benefits that solve THEIR specific problem

### Mistake 6: The Objection Argument
❌ "But our solution is better than what you have!"
✅ "I understand. What would make you consider switching?"

### Mistake 7: The Wishy-Washy CTA
❌ "Maybe sometime we could chat?"
✅ "I have Tuesday at 2pm or Wednesday at 10am. Which works?"

### Mistake 8: The Voicemail Monologue
❌ 45-second VM with full pitch
✅ 15-20 seconds, name + reason + callback number

### Mistake 9: The No-Research Call
❌ Calling with zero context on the prospect
✅ Reference their company, their post, their role

### Mistake 10: The Burnout
❌ Calling 200 times/day then quitting after 2 weeks
✅ Consistent 50-100 calls/day for 6+ months

## PART 13: SCRIPTS FOR SPECIFIC SCENARIOS

### Calling a CEO/Founder

**Tone:** Direct, value-focused, no fluff
**Length:** Shorter (2-3 min)
**Focus:** Business outcomes

**Opener:**
"Hi {{first_name}}, this is {{your_name}}. I help {{type_of_company}} book {{specific_result}}. Saw your {{trigger_event}} at {{company}}. Got 60 seconds?"

**Pitch:**
"We do {{one_sentence_description}}. Average client: {{specific_result}}. Worth 15 minutes?"

### Calling a VP of Sales

**Tone:** Professional, results-focused
**Length:** Standard (3-5 min)
**Focus:** Pipeline, quota, team efficiency

**Opener:**
"Hi {{first_name}}, this is {{your_name}} from {{your_company}}. I help B2B SaaS sales teams book 10-15 qualified demos/month via cold email. Saw {{company}} is hiring AEs. Did I catch you at a bad time?"

**Pitch:**
"Most sales teams we talk to are doing 5-10 demos/month from organic. We help them add 10-15 more from outbound. Average result: 12 demos/month in first 30 days. Worth a 15-min chat?"

### Calling a Marketing Director

**Tone:** Data-driven, ROI-focused
**Length:** Standard (3-5 min)
