Sample emails from the pack

3 examples to show you what you're getting. (Anonymized but representative.)

Email #2: The Specific Observation

Subject: noticed {{company}}'s new {{feature}}

Hey {{first_name}}, Saw {{company}} just launched {{feature}} — congrats. The positioning on the landing page is sharp. One thing I'd be curious about: how are you driving qualified demos to it? Most {{role}} we talk to say distribution is the bottleneck post-launch. We work with SaaS companies on outbound — average 12-15 qualified demos/mo from 1,000 emails. Worth a chat if you're exploring that. {{your_name}}

Why it worked: Specific compliment (not generic), then problem-aware question. The "12-15" number is specific and believable.

Email #5: The Case Study Lead

Subject: {{similar_company}} booked 14 meetings in week 1

Hey {{first_name}}, Quick context: We helped {{similar_company}} (B2B SaaS, {{size}}) book 14 qualified meetings in their first week of cold outreach. They were in a similar spot to {{company}} — {{their_specific_pain}}. The trick wasn't better copy. It was {{your_specific_method}}. Happy to share the case study if useful. {{your_name}}

Why it worked: Names a similar company. The "trick wasn't better copy" is a pattern interrupt.

Email #97: The Breakup

Subject: last email from me

Hey {{first_name}}, I don't want to be that person who keeps emailing. So this is my last one. If cold outreach isn't a priority for {{company}} right now, no worries. But if you ever want to explore it, my calendar's always open: {{calendar_link}}. Best of luck with {{company}}. {{your_name}}

Why it worked: The breakup email paradoxically gets more replies than any other sequence email.
