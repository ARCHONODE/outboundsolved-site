## The Daily 30-Minute Routine

### Minute 0-5: Engage (warm up the algorithm)

LinkedIn shows your content to people you engage with. Spend 5 min liking/commenting on 5-10 posts from:
- SaaS founders you want to reach
- Industry thought leaders in your ICP
- Other agency owners (subtle: build a peer network)

**What to comment:** Add value, not "Great post!" Comments like:
- "We saw the same pattern with our clients — [specific insight]"
- "Curious how you measure [X] for your team"
- "We tried this and got [specific result]"

### Minute 5-20: Send 5-10 connection requests

**Search target profiles, send personalized connection requests.**

Connection request format (300 char limit):

```
Hey {{first_name}}, saw you {{specific_thing_about_them}}.

I run a done-for-you cold email agency for B2B SaaS — using it to 
book my own meetings (loving the dogfood approach).

Would love to connect and swap notes on outbound.
```

**Key rules:**
- NEVER send blank "I'd like to add you to my network"
- ALWAYS reference something specific (their post, their company, their role)
- DO NOT pitch in the connection request — that gets declined 80%+ of the time

### Minute 20-30: Reply to any new connections

When someone accepts your request:
- Wait 5-10 minutes (so the message doesn't feel instant-bot-like)
- Send a thank-you + value-add message
- DON'T pitch in the first message after acceptance

**First message after accept:**

```
Thanks for connecting, {{first_name}}!

Curious — how are you currently handling outbound for {{company}}? 
In-house, agency, or mostly inbound?

(I ask because I'm building something in this space and trying to 
understand how founders are solving it.)
```

**Wait for their reply.** Then continue the conversation naturally. Don't pitch yet.

### Once they reply, follow this conversation flow:

1. **Their reply** → you respond with a specific insight or question
2. **3-5 messages in** → if conversation is going well, soft CTA: "Happy to show you what we built. 15 min?"
3. **They say yes** → send Calendly link
4. **They say no** → "No worries. If you ever want to swap notes on outbound, my door's open."

## The 4 Message Types (rotate through these)

### 1. The Insight Share
"Thought of you when I saw this — [link to article/post]. The [specific takeaway] is exactly what we see in our client work."

### 2. The Genuine Question
"Curious how you think about [specific topic] for {{company}}. We've been wrestling with this for our clients."

### 3. The Congratulation
"Saw {{company}} just {{specific_thing}}. Congrats! Curious what's next on the roadmap?"

### 4. The Soft Ask (only after rapport)
"Hey, full disclosure — I run a done-for-you cold email agency. Not pitching, but if you're ever exploring that, would love to chat. No pressure either way."

**The ratio:** For every 1 "soft ask", send 5+ insight/question/congratulation messages. This builds trust.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Pitching in the connection request** → 80%+ decline rate
2. **Sending generic "I'd like to add you to my network"** → instant delete
3. **Following up too fast** → wait 3 days minimum
4. **Following up too many times** → 2-3 max, then move on
5. **Pitching in the first message** → builds no rapport
6. **Sending 50 connection requests in one day** → LinkedIn rate-limits + flags as spam
7. **Commenting "Great post!" on everything** → looks fake, tanks your profile
8. **Talking only about your service** → talk about THEM, their problems
9. **Lying or exaggerating** → small LinkedIn world, will catch up
10. **Giving up after week 1** → takes 2-3 weeks to see real results
